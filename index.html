About Ventspils

Ventspils is a city in northwestern Latvia in the historical Courland region of Latvia, and is the sixth largest city in the country.

The city is a major Baltic port and has been an important trading hub since the Hanseatic period. Today, it's known for its clean beaches, family-friendly attractions, and rich cultural heritage.

Quick Facts
  • Population: ~40,000
  • Location: Northwestern Latvia
  • Founded: 1290
  • Features: Port City, Beaches

Rich History

Medieval Period

Founded in 1290 by the Livonian Order, Ventspils grew as a important Hanseatic port.

19th Century

Industrial revolution brought rapid development and expansion of the port.

Modern Era

Today, Ventspils is a modern city combining historical heritage with contemporary amenities.

Top Attractions

Ventspils Castle

The oldest medieval fortress in Latvia, built by the Livonian Order.

Blue Flag Beach

A pristine white sand beach perfect for summer activities.

Narrow Gauge Railway

Historical railway offering scenic rides along the coast.
